## Prompt

Begin from <<<image_1>>> and animate toward <<<image_2>>>.
<<<image_3>>> is a loose style reference for the characters —
the model has full creative freedom on motion and timing.

STYLE: 1930s Fleischer Brothers rubber hose animation. Hand-drawn
ink outlines on vintage parchment. Noodly boneless limbs with no
elbows or knees. Constant fluid deformation — characters squash,
stretch, and bounce like they're made of rubber. Oversized white
gloves, pie-cut eyes, big toothy grins. Warm sepia and ivory palette
with pops of lime green. Watercolor grain texture. 24fps hand-drawn
feel. Everything is alive — even inanimate objects breathe, wobble,
and bop to an invisible rhythm.

PHASE 1 — EMPTY (0-1s): The empty badge sits still. The airplane
propeller starts spinning. The badge wobbles slightly, anticipating.

PHASE 2 — CHAOS (1-3.5s): Cartoon characters burst in from all
sides — bouncing, tumbling, cartwheeling. Full rubber hose physics.
They overshoot, bounce back, bonk into each other. Playful and messy.
They gradually find spots around the badge circle.

PHASE 3 — LOCK (3.5-5s): Everything settles into the final badge
composition from <<<image_2>>>. One warm pulse of golden light.
All elements gently spinning. Alive and complete.

Sound design (SFX only): Cartoon whooshes, spring boings on landings,
soft mechanical hum of spinning parts, one satisfying click as the
badge completes.
