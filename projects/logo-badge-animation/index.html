STYLE: 1930s Fleischer Brothers rubber hose animation. Hand-drawn ink outlines on vintage parchment paper. Noodly boneless limbs, no elbows or knees. Extreme squash and stretch. Oversized white gloves, pie-cut eyes, big toothy grins. Warm sepia and ivory with pops of lime green. Watercolor grain. Everything alive. A circular vintage badge logo with a cartoon airplane in the center starts empty and still. Then it comes to life: The propeller spins faster and faster. Suddenly, wild cartoon characters — a bouncing windmill, a spinning gear, a dancing disco ball with sunglasses, a waddling washing machine with clothes flying out, a cheerful helicopter buzzing overhead, a wobbling globe with a magnifying glass, a marching hourglass, a grooving record player, and a dizzy ferris wheel — all come tumbling, bouncing, rolling, and flying into frame from every direction. Full rubber hose chaos. They gradually spiral into position around the circular badge. A warm golden pulse. Everything gently spins. Complete and alive. SFX only — cartoon whooshes, rubbery boings, spring sounds, mechanical whirring, one satisfying metallic click.
